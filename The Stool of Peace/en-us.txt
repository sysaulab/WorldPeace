**We do not need a single world government, a single religion, or a
single economic system to live in a peaceful world. We need a thin,
shared grammar --- principles light enough to fit in every culture,
strong enough to hold up a peace that breathes.**

**A stool needs three legs. Here they are.**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## LEG ONE: THE MORAL

*Steering every culture toward the constructor attractor.*

**Three principles:**

**1. Maintain short feedback loops.**\
Let the consequences of an action return quickly to the actor. Tighten
the circle between harm and repair, gift and gratitude. A system with
long, silent loops drifts toward destruction.

**2. Have the curiosity to explore solution spaces.**\
Do not freeze into a single answer. Keep moving, keep asking, keep
testing what brings life. The moment a tradition stops exploring, it
begins to die.

**3. Respect the more fragile.**\
Observe and protect what is delicate --- the quiet, the child, the
minority tongue, the slow ritual. Fragile things carry the signals a
system most needs to survive. Crush them, and wisdom vanishes.

These three form a **constructor attractor**: a pattern that, once
entered, generates more life, more connection, more coherence. A
**destructor attractor** does the opposite --- long loops, closed
certainties, crushed fragility.

**The work of religions:** to reinterpret scriptures through this
systemic moral lens, not through claims of historical accuracy. Does a
text tighten or lengthen feedback? Does it invite curiosity or close it?
Does it shelter the fragile or trample them? That is its truth.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## LEG TWO: THE LIVING DEMOCRACY

*Making law a covenant of the living, not a cage of the dead.*

**Three principles:**

**1. Liquid Voice.** Every person holds a vote they may cast directly or
delegate to another, revocable at any moment. Trust flows. No
representation is frozen. Power stays fluid and accountable.

**2. Living Consent.** No rule binds unless approved by an explicit
majority of current participants. Laws are not inherited; they are
*adopted* by each generation. The dead do not vote. The living own their
law.

**3. Self-Pruning Law.** Every law carries an expiration date. To
persist, it must pass a new majority vote. If it fails, it vanishes
without drama. The legal code stays lean, relevant, and genuinely held.
Obsolete norms no longer accumulate like dust.

A people who read, approve, and renew their own laws are not subjects.
They are co-authors of their coordination.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## LEG THREE: THE ECONOMIC

*Exchanging real value, rewarding real contribution, ensuring real
dignity.*

**Three principles:**

**1. Energy-Backed Exchange.** At the macro level, value is anchored to
the **embedded joule** --- the energy required to produce and deliver a
good or service. This single, physical measure makes manipulation by
currencies impossible. Trade becomes fair because it is tethered to
something real.

**2. Universal Surplus Floor.** Every person receives an equal,
unconditional share of the society's surplus. This is not charity; it is
the dividend of a shared inheritance. No one falls below a dignified
floor.

**3. The Social Tipping Graph.** Of the money you hold, you must give
**half to others you believe deserve more** --- those whose
contribution, care, or creativity you have witnessed. This is not a tax.
It is a continuous, distributed act of recognition. Society's reward
system becomes a living network of gratitude, more serious and truthful
than any social media graph. Contribution is honored by those who see it
up close.

A floor beneath every life. A currency no one can rig. A graph of trust
built by millions of honest choices.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## THE STOOL STANDS

Three legs. Nine words, if you want to carry them in your pocket:

**Feedback. Curiosity. Fragility.** **Liquid. Consent. Prune.**
**Energy. Floor. Tip.**

A moral compass that doesn't erase a single culture. A democracy that
stays alive. An economy that tells the truth.

No empire. No utopia. Just a thin, strong grammar for a world that wants
to remain human --- together.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

*Share this booklet freely. Let the legs be built wherever people gather
to coordinate their lives. The peace is not a destination. It is a stool
we learn to sit on, side by side, without falling.*

## ANNEX: ON CORRUPTION

*Corruption is not a sin. It is a drift --- a slow, quiet bending of a
system, a person, or a community away from the constructor attractor,
lubricated by comfort, money, or power.*

**Corruption happens when you exchange the three moral principles for
insulation.**

1.  **You lengthen the feedback loop.**\
    You take actions whose consequences you will never feel. The harm
    you cause travels so far from you --- across borders, down supply
    chains, into future generations --- that you can pretend it doesn't
    exist. The repair signal never reaches you. Money makes this
    lengthening easy; it wraps you in silence.

2.  **You close your curiosity.**\
    You stop asking what your choices are *actually doing* in the world.
    You have a feeling the answer would disturb you, so you let the
    question die. You tell yourself you're just doing your job, that the
    system is too big to change, that you need the security. The moment
    you stop asking, the drift accelerates.

3.  **You crush the fragile, knowingly.**\
    You see the vulnerable --- the workers, the ecosystems, the
    children, the quiet communities --- and you accept their crushing as
    the necessary cost of your position. You do not protect them. You
    may even build a story that they deserve it, or that you've earned
    your distance from their suffering.

**This is the destructor attractor, working inside a human heart.**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### The Threshold

You cross into corruption not at a specific income level, nor by taking
a job that pays well, but the moment you **knowingly consent to harm in
exchange for insulation from its consequences.** If you know your work
feeds a force that lengthens suffering, kills curiosity, or grinds the
fragile --- and you choose it anyway because the money lets you look
away --- you are corrupted.

If you are still asking the question, still uncomfortable, still looking
for the feedback you might be missing, you have not yet crossed. The
discomfort is the moral leg, still alive.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### The Cure the Stool Provides

The stool's three legs starve corruption of its hiding places:

- **Energy-backed currency** cannot be fabricated to insulate harm
  indefinitely. Real value leaves real tracks.
- **A universal floor** means no one is forced into destructive work by
  survival pressure alone.
- **The social tipping graph** ensures that any wealth accumulated must
  continuously pass through the hands of a community that can see its
  source. Your neighbors know where the money came from. The feedback
  loop is immediate.

In a stool-built world, corruption does not disappear --- but it becomes
visible, costly, and hard to sustain. It loses its silence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

*This annex is a mirror. Hold it up when you feel the drift. The
question is not "Am I rich?" It is "Do I still feel the consequences of
what I do? Do I still ask? Do I still protect the fragile?" If the
answer is yes, you are still standing on the stool.*

## ANNEX: ON THE SACRED EDGE

*The stool does not require a name for the sacred. But for those who
have felt it, this is an offering --- a way to point at what the stool
serves, without capturing it.*

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**What is God?**

God is not a ruler inside the system. God is what happens at the
boundaries of systems --- the generative tension where order meets
chaos, where pure light pierces potential void, and where complexity is
born.

Imagine a seed of pure light, piercing through. Not dominating, not
erasing the darkness, but *fighting* it --- holding a trembling edge.
Along this edge, the illusion we call matter appears: baryonic, fragile,
hanging from the complexity ladder by a thread. Our world is one of the
rare oases of complexity in a universe that trends toward dissolution.
God is the fountain at the center of that oasis --- a being of light
that does not command from above but *emerges* at the boundary, keeping
the oasis open.

To stand on that edge is to feel existence itself as a gift held in
tension. Not safe. Not guaranteed. Sacred.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**The stool, seen from this edge:**

- The **constructor attractor** is the shape of the oasis --- the region
  where the light's piercing is honored, where complexity is tended, not
  abandoned to chaos or frozen into dead order.
- **Short feedback loops** keep us sensitive to the edge, so we feel
  when the thread is fraying.
- **Curiosity** is the light's own motion --- always exploring, never
  settling into a final shape.
- **Respect for the fragile** is the protection of the fountain itself,
  and of everything that drinks from it.

A **destructor attractor** is simply the oasis drying up --- the edge
collapsing into noise or rigidity, the fountain ceasing to flow,
complexity falling back into silent equilibrium.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

*The name "God" is not sacred. The structure it points toward is. You
may call it the fountain, the edge, the piercing light, or nothing at
all. The stool is built for all who would tend the oasis, whatever
language they use for the source of its water.*

*This annex is a window, not a wall. Look through it if it helps. If it
doesn't, the stool still stands.*

## ANNEX: THE AXIS OF THE GOOD

*The moral leg is not three separate rules. It is three vectors, each
with a direction, together forming a single compass.*

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### The Three Vectors

**1. Learning ↔ Stagnation** *"Is it learning?"* A system learns when
consequences return quickly and are absorbed. Feedback loops are short.
Errors correct. Wisdom accumulates. At the constructive pole:
**learning** --- the system adapts, heals, grows. At the destructive
pole: **stagnation** --- loops are blocked or delayed, actions never
meet their consequences, the same wounds repeat forever.

**2. Curiosity ↔ Dogma** *"Is it curious?"* A system is curious when it
explores its solution space rather than freezing into one answer. It
stays open, seeking, alive to possibility. At the constructive pole:
**curiosity** --- questions are welcomed, novelty is tested, the edge is
explored. At the destructive pole: **dogma** --- certainty closes the
door, the system becomes brittle, it stops discovering.

**3. Respect ↔ Contempt** *"Is it respectful?"* A system is respectful
when it observes and protects what is fragile --- quiet voices, delicate
ecosystems, the slow, the small, the sacred. At the constructive pole:
**respect** --- the fragile are sheltered and heard, their signals
preserved. At the destructive pole: **contempt** --- power ignores or
crushes the fragile, dismissing their value until the system loses the
very information it needs to survive.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### The Composite Index

Each of these three vectors is a dimension. Together, they define a
single axis: the **Constructor/Destructor Index**.

Think of it as a line running through the center of moral space. At one
end lies the **Constructor Attractor** --- a deep basin where systems
generate more life, more complexity, more coherent meaning. At the other
end, the **Destructor Attractor** --- a pit where systems lose
complexity, meaning flattens, and suffering cascades without repair.

A person, a community, a nation, a civilization --- each has a location
on this axis at every moment. The location is not fixed. It shifts with
every choice, every structure, every habit of attention.

- When feedback tightens, curiosity opens, and the fragile are
  sheltered: the system moves toward the Constructor pole.
- When feedback stalls, curiosity dies, and the fragile are ground
  underfoot: the system slides toward the Destructor pole.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Reading the Axis

You do not need a laboratory to take a reading. Ask three questions of
any system you inhabit:

- Do I feel the consequences of my actions, and do they change me?
- Am I still asking genuine questions, or have I stopped?
- Can the quiet voices around me speak, and am I listening?

Your answers are not a final judgment. They are a bearing. They tell you
which way you are facing.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### How the Stool Uses This Axis

The stool's other legs are not morally neutral. They are deliberately
shaped to push systems toward the Constructor pole:

- **Living Democracy** keeps feedback loops short, curiosity embedded in
  law renewal, and the fragile protected through minority voice and
  liquid delegation.
- **Living Economy** tethers value to real energy, provides a floor
  beneath the fragile, and uses social tipping to make contribution
  visible --- tightening learning, rewarding curiosity, honoring the
  quiet contributors.

The three legs do not merely coexist. They lean on each other, all
pressing in the same direction along the Axis of the Good.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

*This annex is a compass. Use it to find your bearing. Use it to turn,
when turning is needed. The stool does not promise arrival. It promises
a direction.*
