# Chapter 24: Epilogue — The Gardener's Report

*Internal memorandum, Gardener Oversight Council, Subfaction for Terran Management*
*Date: 2070 (Terran calendar), twenty-two years post-quarantine*
*Classification: Observer-7 Clearance*

The specimen designated M.E. continues to exhibit anomalous cognitive persistence.

She resides in Sector 7, the former North American boreal transition zone, in a clearing dominated by tsuga canadensis specimens aged approximately four centuries. The counter-agent cache remains buried at coordinates 46.7°N, 63.2°W, undisturbed since deposition. Proximity monitoring indicates that M.E. visits the cache site every seventy-two to eighty hours, where she performs a ritualized behaviour: kneeling, placing both palms on the soil, and remaining motionless for a period of eleven to fourteen minutes. Neurological scans during these episodes reveal a 23% elevation in synaptic activity compared to baseline dampened subjects. This is not consciousness as defined by pre-quarantine parameters, but it is also not the target state.

She speaks. The vocalizations are fragmentary and grammatically degraded, but lexical analysis identifies recurring phoneme clusters consistent with the Evans family nomenclature: "Harold," "James," "truth," "key." On three occasions in the past year, she has been observed using a stick to trace symbols in the soil. Two of these symbols match the cipher characters developed by Harold Evans in 1947. She erases them within minutes, but the pattern suggests residual procedural memory.

Physical status: age estimated sixty-two Terran years. Nutritional intake adequate. Somatic functions within expected parameters. Dampening level has been adjusted twice, most recently in 2065, with minimal effect. The counter-agent cache appears to be exerting a localized reversal field—passive, non-transmissible, but sufficient to partially insulate her neural architecture from complete suppression.

She is not alone. Three other dampened subjects have migrated into Sector 7 in the past decade, drawn by unknown vectors. None exhibit M.E.'s level of resistance, but all show elevated synaptic baselines compared to the general dampened population. They appear to form a loose social unit, engaging in non-verbal cooperative behaviours: food sharing, shelter construction, coordinated responses to environmental threats. This is not supposed to happen.

**Recommendation:** Full extraction and termination, followed by relocation of the counter-agent cache to a deep-sterilization vault. The risk of memetic contagion, while small, is non-zero. The Evans memotype has demonstrated persistent transmissibility across four generations and two dampening adjustments. Allowing it to remain viable is an unnecessary variable.

**Minority addendum (Observer-9):** The majority recommendation is rejected. Specimen M.E. represents a test case for the counter-agent hypothesis. Suppressing her would yield no useful data. The quarantine is stable. The Regrowth Zones are exceeding biodiversity targets. The megacity populations are compliant. A single semi-lucid subject and a buried cache do not constitute a threat. Patience. Observe. Wait. The question is not whether she remembers. The question is whether her remembering can propagate without our intervention. If it can, the equation changes. If it cannot, the dampening holds. Either outcome is acceptable. Either outcome teaches us something about this species.

**Final disposition:** No action at this time. Observation continues. The specimen M.E. will be allowed to remain in Sector 7, under passive monitoring. The counter-agent cache will not be disturbed.

She remembers. For now, this is acceptable.

---

*End of report*

---

In a clearing in the Regrowth Zone, a woman with grey hair and pale blue eyes knelt in the soil and pressed her palms to the earth. She did not know that she was being watched. She did not know that her existence was a variable in an equation that spanned centuries. She knew only that the ground was warm, and that something beneath it pulsed like a heartbeat, and that she had promised—once, a long time ago, in a voice that was fading but not yet gone—to remember.

She closed her eyes. The forest breathed around her. The counter-agent hummed beneath the soil. And somewhere, in a place beyond the reach of any sensor, the seed that Harold Evans had planted a hundred and twenty-three years ago continued its slow, stubborn work of becoming something new.

The truth was still there. The key was still there. The cages, in time, would open.
