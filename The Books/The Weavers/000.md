
## Proposed Expanded Structure for *The Weavers of Səlilwət*

### Prologue: The Last Days of the Old World
- The dopamine crash of 2031 (drawn from *History of a Dopamine Rush*)
- The shelter system as a machine of demoralization
- Leo’s decision to walk out into the rain

### Part One: Oppenheimer Park (Year 0)
- **Chapter 1:** Leo pitches his tent. First encounters: the woman who sweeps the sidewalk, the chess players, the children.
- **Chapter 2:** Mira finds him on the log. The conversation about Hegel and potlatches.
- **Chapter 3:** The loading bay meeting. Oso’s stew. Kiah’s laptop. Dev’s soldering iron.
- **Chapter 4:** The first constitution – Leo writes the six rules. Debate about the tip economy.
- **Chapter 5:** The first scarf. The elderly woman. Leo’s realization about recognition.

### Side Sketches: The Oppenheimer Suburbans – Season 1
- **Sketch 1:** Morning roll call (camping chairs, instant coffee, the air pump)
- **Sketch 2:** The Marathon & the booster pack (Sylvain’s car won’t start, road service, Canadian Tire)
- **Sketch 3:** The SPCA incident (two‑hour negotiation, Beans, Toast, Gimpy)
- **Sketch 4:** Randall’s headphones break (Sylvain finds a soldering iron, Dev from the trust helps)
- **Sketch 5:** Serena registers Gimpy as “morale animal” (bylaw officer ends up staying for dinner)
- **Sketch 6:** The Guy fixes his truck with a potato (it almost works; Sylvain is not impressed)
- **Sketch 7:** Animal control returns – the “thirty‑day follow‑up” (Serena has printed business cards)
- **Sketch 8:** A city councillor visits the parking lot (she comes to ticket, leaves promising to fund a portable toilet)

### Part Two: The First Trust (Years 1–3)
- **Chapter 6:** Legalizing the squat – the Community Investment Trust structure
- **Chapter 7:** Buying the SROs for a dollar each. The rot. The work.
- **Chapter 8:** The tip economy in action – data from Mira’s research assistant
- **Chapter 9:** The first copycat trust (New Westminster). Kiah travels to help.
- **Chapter 10:** The potlatch ceremony – Indigenous elders bless the trust

### Part Three: The Withdrawal Years (Years 4–10)
- **Chapter 11:** Outside the trust – societal collapse accelerates (dopamine withdrawal symptoms)
- **Chapter 12:** The trust as a boring superpower (cortisol vs. oxytocin study)
- **Chapter 13:** The Coast Salish Economic Circle – no central committee
- **Chapter 14:** Leo’s transformation – from philosopher to scribe to elder
- **Chapter 15:** The first generation born inside the trust

### Side Sketches: The Oppenheimer Suburbans – Season 2 (Years 4–10)
- **Sketch 9:** The parking lot gets solar panels (Sylvain’s idea, The Guy’s labour)
- **Sketch 10:** Randall speaks (first full sentence in three years – “The soup is good”)
- **Sketch 11:** Serena starts a mobile pet‑sitting service (clients include trust members)
- **Sketch 12:** The Guy’s truck finally dies (the parking lot holds a funeral)
- **Sketch 13:** A new neighbour arrives – a retired nurse in a converted school bus
- **Sketch 14:** The bylaw officer returns – not to ticket, but to ask for advice on starting a trust

### Part Four: The National Turn (Years 11–15)
- **Chapter 16:** The crash of 2040 – banks fail, government gutted
- **Chapter 17:** The bulk transfer deal – Səlilwət grows to 20,000
- **Chapter 18:** The CoReHubs memorandum (Fountain’s document) – how it circulates
- **Chapter 19:** The pilot program – five cities, five hubs, underground parking factories
- **Chapter 20:** The scramble capability – drills, three‑process operators, defense contracts
- **Chapter 21:** The PIN is built – cryptography, news backbone, Memorial Register
- **Chapter 22:** The thin ethical framework – non‑supremacy, recognition, transparency

### Part Five: The Gathering Storm (Years 16–20)
- **Chapter 23:** The United States fractures (dopamine withdrawal without the trust)
- **Chapter 24:** The invasion of 2050 – American armoured thrust into Alberta
- **Chapter 25:** The scramble – hubs go to 100% capacity
- **Chapter 26:** Civilian drones, encrypted radios, modular clean rooms
- **Chapter 27:** The withdrawal – American casualties, Canadian resilience
- **Chapter 28:** The aftermath – a broken continent to the south

### Side Sketches: The Oppenheimer Suburbans – Season 3 (The War Years)
- **Sketch 15:** The parking lot becomes a lookout (Randall’s hearing detects distant engines)
- **Sketch 16:** Serena’s Jeep becomes a mobile field hospital (Gimpy the therapy dog works overtime)
- **Sketch 17:** Sylvain’s booster pack saves a stranded ambulance
- **Sketch 18:** The Guy’s new truck (a hybrid) is commandeered for supply runs – he drives like a maniac, but everything arrives
- **Sketch 19:** A wounded American soldier wanders into the parking lot. They give him soup. He stays.

### Part Six: The Reconstruction (Years 21–50)
- **Chapter 29:** The Weaver Missions – Kiah leads the first trust into Seattle
- **Chapter 30:** The Pacific Republic adopts the model – contagion, not conquest
- **Chapter 31:** The Atlantic Federation fails (top‑down socialism)
- **Chapter 32:** The Texas Commonwealth fails (free‑market fundamentalism)
- **Chapter 33:** The PIN goes global – every community joins or is left behind
- **Chapter 34:** The death of supremacist ideologies – exclusion, not violence
- **Chapter 35:** The emergence of meta‑culture (Gift Exchange Protocol, Dispute Resolution Commons, Learning Journey)

### Part Seven: The Long Plateau (Years 51–100)
- **Chapter 36:** Mira dies – the first elder passes. Her great‑grandchildren carry on.
- **Chapter 37:** Dev’s last invention – a self‑repairing knitting machine
- **Chapter 38:** Oso’s kitchen becomes a teaching institute
- **Chapter 39:** Leo writes the final edition of the constitution – it is still one page
- **Chapter 40:** The Səlilwət towers – vertical villages, thematic floors, elevators as social glue
- **Chapter 41:** The tip economy at scale – metastable dynamics, no permanent hierarchy

### Side Sketches: The Oppenheimer Suburbans – Season 4 (The Plateau)
- **Sketch 20:** The parking lot becomes a permanent tiny home village (legalized)
- **Sketch 21:** Randall starts a podcast (episode 1: “The Sound of Silence” – 3 hours of ambient noise)
- **Sketch 22:** Serena opens a small animal sanctuary (funded by the trust)
- **Sketch 23:** Sylvain becomes the parking lot’s unofficial historian (his notes become a book)
- **Sketch 24:** The Guy’s granddaughter visits – she is a student at the trust’s engineering school
- **Sketch 25:** The original air pump is donated to the Səlilwət museum

### Epilogue: The Fountain’s Return (Year 100)
- The centenary ceremony – Kiah’s great‑great‑granddaughter speaks
- The original knitting machine, still functional
- A grey‑haired figure at the back of the crowd – Fountain, the author of the CoReHubs memorandum
- Leo, now very old, recognizes them. They nod. No words are needed.
- The final scarf is knitted – given to a newborn.

### Coda: The Open Question
- What kind of civilization emerges?
- The substrate is ready. The network is live.
- **What will you weave?**
