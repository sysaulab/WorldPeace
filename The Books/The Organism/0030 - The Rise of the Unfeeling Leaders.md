# Chapter 3: The Rise of the Unfeeling Leaders

A strange thing happens inside a sick body. It starts to reward the wrong people.

In any group of people, some are sensitive. They notice when others are hurting. They feel bad when they cause harm. They hesitate before making a choice that might damage someone else. These people are the body's natural healers. They are the ones who keep feedback loops short.

But in a sick body—a body that worships the Money idol and hides behind the membrane—these sensitive people are pushed aside. They are too slow. They care too much. They ask too many questions. They are told: *This is just business. Don't take it personally. You need to toughen up.*

Instead, the body raises up a different type of person. The type who can make hard decisions without losing sleep. The type who can fire thousands of workers and call it efficiency. The type who can order a bombing and call it strategy. The type who can look at a dying forest and see only timber value.

These people are not monsters. Most of them started out normal. But the system shaped them. It rewarded them for turning off their empathy. It punished them for keeping it on. Over time, the body's leaders become a population that is very good at winning the money game and very bad at feeling the pain they cause.

The book calls this the *psychopathic gradient*.

Psychopathy is a condition where a person cannot feel what others feel. They can be charming. They can be smart. But they lack the inner voice that says, *Stop. You are hurting someone.* In a healthy body, this condition is rare and recognized as a problem. In the sick body, it becomes the norm for people in power.

The gradient works like this. A billionaire's ruthlessness is called business acumen. A politician's manipulation is called political skill. A CEO's lack of empathy is called tough decision-making. The culture starts to praise exactly the traits that, in a friend or a partner, would make you run away.

And because the leaders shape the culture, and the culture shapes the leaders, the cycle spins faster. The body becomes more psychopathic from the top down. Empathy becomes a weakness. Cruelty becomes a strength.

The most frightening part? The body loses the ability to see this as a sickness at all. It tells itself: *This is just how the world works. This is human nature. The strong survive. The weak fall behind.*

The body is now hurting itself on purpose and calling it wisdom. The nerves that would scream *stop* have been cut. The people who would scream *stop* have been told they are the crazy ones.

The sickness has become the story the body tells itself about what health looks like.
