# Chapter 1: A Body That Cannot Feel

Imagine a giant living body. It is so big that no single cell inside it can see where it begins or ends. This body is not made of flesh and bone. It is made of people—billions of them—and the connections between them. Their families, their workplaces, their governments, their shared beliefs. This body is humanity. And it is sick.

The sickness does not start with a fever or a wound you can see. It starts with numbness. The body does something, but it cannot feel what that action does. It reaches for something it thinks will help—a new gadget, a new war, a new law—and feels nothing. No satisfaction. No pain. Just a quick flash of wanting, then emptiness. So it reaches again. And again.

A healthy body learns from its actions. You touch fire, you pull your hand back. You eat something bad, you throw it up. The message travels fast. Action leads to consequence. Consequence leads to change.

A sick body loses that connection. The nerves stop working. The signals get blocked. The body hurts itself and does not know it is doing so.

Watch what this sick body does. It eats up soil and calls it growth. It drains rivers and lakes and calls it progress. It burns forests and calls it energy. It puts its own children to work in dangerous jobs, sends them to fight in wars, feeds them to machines of profit and distraction—and when they cry out, the larger body hears nothing. Just noise. Static.

The pain is real. But it goes nowhere. The hand does not feel the fist it throws. The mouth does not taste the poison it drinks.

This is the sickness: the breaking of feedback loops. Not by accident. On purpose. Over time. Built into the systems we live inside.
