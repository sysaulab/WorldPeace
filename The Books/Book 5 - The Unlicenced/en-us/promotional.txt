**Description**

*A man with no past. A world on the brink. An offer no one can refuse—and a refusal no one can forget.*

He wakes up on a park bench in Queens with no memory, no ID, and a paper hospital bracelet bearing a name he doesn’t recognize: Joshua Carpenter. He has no plan, no possessions, and no explanation for his presence. But he has something far more unsettling: a quiet, unwavering capacity to see the people the world has rendered invisible.

He washes the feet of the homeless. He dines with sex workers and sex offenders. He carries a paralyzed veteran on his back through the streets of the Bronx. He walks uninvited into a psychiatric ward, a billionaire’s gala, and a televised interview that leaves a notorious talk-show host weeping on air. He asks for nothing. He builds no organization. He simply pays attention—and attention, in a world starved of it, becomes the most disruptive force imaginable.

As his quiet acts ripple outward, the systems of power begin to take notice. A federal analyst is dispatched to observe him. A religious council denounces him. A secret intelligence dossier labels him a “memetic contagion,” a nonviolent threat to the social order. And when global tensions push the world’s superpowers to the brink of war, he is thrust into the one arena he never sought: the United Nations General Assembly, where the leaders of every nation demand he take a side.

His response will not be what anyone expects.

*The Unlicensed* is a luminous, provocative novel of ideas—a story of quiet subversion, radical compassion, and the terrifying possibility that the change the world needs might not come from power, but from presence. Perfect for readers of Shūsaku Endō, Ursula K. Le Guin, and Marilynne Robinson, this book offers no easy answers, only the unsettling, lingering question: *What would happen if someone finally saw you—all of you—and refused to look away?*
