# The Poor Party

We are poor. You are rich.

That is the only identity that matters.

You have spent decades teaching us that our differences matter more than our common condition. You taught the white poor to hate the Black poor. You taught the Christian poor to fear the Muslim poor. You taught the native-born poor to blame the immigrant poor. You taught men that women were the enemy. You taught straight that gay was the threat. You taught cis that trans was the danger.

You did this because you knew: if we ever looked up and saw each other, we would see you.

And we have seen you.

We see you in your penthouses while we sleep in doorways.
We see you in your private jets while we breathe your exhaust.
We see you in your gated communities while our neighborhoods are policed like occupied territories.
We see you in your boardrooms deciding which of us gets to eat this month.
We see you in your parliaments passing laws that protect your wealth and call it fiscal responsibility.
We see you in your wars—always our children dying, never yours.
We see you in your diversity campaigns, your rainbow logos, your Black Lives Matter signs, your land acknowledgements—all the costumes you wear while the machinery grinds on unchanged.

You have women among you now. You have gay people. You have Black people. You have Indigenous people. You have trans people. You have disabled people.

And you are still rich. And we are still poor.

You did not join us. You made us join you. You offered a few of our faces a seat at your table while the rest of us starved outside the restaurant.

**You are not our representatives. You are our landlords. Our bosses. Our creditors. Our warmongers.**

What percentage of your parliaments, your congresses, your boards, your cabinets, your courts are poor? What percentage have ever been poor? What percentage grew up hungry? Watched their parents die from treatable diseases? Lost their homes? Worked three jobs and still couldn't make rent? Been evicted? Been foreclosed? Been garnished? Been homeless? 

**Democracy means representation.**

If half of us are poor, half of you should be poor. If 40% of us are one missed paycheck from disaster, 40% of you should know what that feels like. If our children are hungry, your children should be hungry until our children are fed.

That is democracy. Not this. Not a ruling class that recruits a few of us to wear your uniforms and speak your language and protect your wealth while calling it progress. We are not asking for a seat at the table, we are taking over the kitchen.

The gap between us is growing. The bridge is getting longer. The hand will not stay open forever. We have been patient for centuries. We have waited for reforms that never came. We have trusted leaders who betrayed us. We have voted for change that never arrived. We have hoped for justice that was always just around the corner. That patience is exhausted.

We are the 99%, we are taking power back.

**Walk with us or get out of the way.**
