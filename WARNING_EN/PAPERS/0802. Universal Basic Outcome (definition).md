
# Universal Basic Outcome (UBO)

## Definition

UBO is a socio-economic framework that guarantees a set of essential life outcomes through direct provision, while distributing an ecologically bounded surplus equally to all citizens, with a structural requirement that half of that surplus be voluntarily allocated to others as an act of recognition, thereby transforming gratitude into the primary distributive mechanism of post-scarcity abundance.

---

### I. The Outcome Core (Direct Provision Guarantee)

A fixed, democratically determined fraction of society’s productive capacity is permanently ring-fenced to deliver the following as non-negotiable, universal outcomes, in-kind:

- **Shelter** – safe, stable, dignified housing
- **Nourishment** – nutritionally adequate, culturally appropriate food
- **Health** – comprehensive physical and mental healthcare
- **Education** – lifelong access to learning and skill development

These are provisioned directly, not via vouchers or cash transfers, to immunize them against market failures, price volatility, and inflationary erosion. The Outcome Core constitutes the material floor of human dignity.

---

### II. The Ecological Surplus Dividend

**Definition.**  
The surplus is the difference between total sustainable productive output and the resources consumed by the Outcome Core, capped by planetary boundaries. It is measured in real terms (energy, materials, labor) and translated into a per-capita dividend of purchasing power or consumption credits.

**Ecological Governor.**  
The permissible size of the surplus is governed by a strict ecological rule:

> Surplus extraction shall remain at minimal levels until key biodiversity indicators (e.g., species abundance, habitat integrity) show sustained recovery. Once recovery is confirmed, the surplus cap may be gradually relaxed, always subject to the primacy of ecological stability.

The dividend is distributed **equally and unconditionally** to every person on Earth. It is the universal share of what the planet can safely provide beyond basic needs.

---

### III. The Recognition Redistribution Mandate

**Rule.**  
Every recipient of the surplus dividend **must allocate exactly 50% of that dividend to other individuals or recognized collective entities**. The remaining 50% is retained for discretionary personal use.

**Allocation Criteria.**  
Recipients are free to distribute their mandatory half to any person(s) or cause(s) they genuinely recognize as:

- Contributing positively to their well-being or that of the community,
- Possessing and executing good ideas,
- Demonstrating kindness, care, or general prosocial character.

This is a **voluntary but structurally required act of gratitude**. It is not a tax; the recipient retains full agency over the choice of recipient.

**Operational Features.**
- **Proximity Bias Expected, Not Enforced.** Most recognition will likely flow to those in one’s direct community—neighbors, caregivers, local volunteers. Reputation can extend recognition beyond personal acquaintance.
- **Charity Bypass Permitted.** The mandate can be fulfilled by gifting to a recognized charitable organization, but this produces a diffuse signal and may neglect local, visible contributors, creating a soft incentive toward direct interpersonal giving.
- **Closed-Loop Reciprocity Allowed, Self-Limiting.** Two parties may exchange their 50% allocations symmetrically, retaining the full value. However, this generates zero new social capital, isolates them from the broader recognition graph, and leaves them invisible—a perfectly acceptable opt-out for those who wish to minimize social engagement (e.g., some autistic individuals, introverts, or those in retreat).
- **No Minimum Engagement.** The Outcome Core remains untouched regardless of participation in the recognition game. The mandate applies only to the surplus, preserving absolute material security.

---

### IV. The Social Graph of Recognition

Each act of mandated giving is recorded, creating a dynamic, weighted directed graph of appreciation.

**Functions.**
1. **Identification of Isolation.** Aggregated, anonymized metrics reveal individuals who consistently give or receive little, enabling voluntary community outreach without coercion.
2. **Emergent Reputation.** Nodes with high, diverse incoming recognition accrue social capital, surfacing valued contributors organically.
3. **Anti-Calcification.** The requirement to actively choose recipients compels continuous social engagement, preventing withdrawal into passive consumerism. A society of overabundance is kept fluid by the constant circulation of gratitude.

**Privacy.**  
The graph’s architecture must employ privacy-preserving technologies (e.g., zero-knowledge proofs, differential privacy) such that personal networks are not surveilled, while public health insights (isolation metrics) and voluntary reputation signals remain accessible.

---

### V. The Aristocracy of the Appreciated

The recognition function naturally produces a hierarchy of esteem. This is not a bug but the system’s core dynamic:

- **Non-hereditary and non-coercive.** All status is earned daily through the free choices of others.
- **Positive-sum.** Competing for recognition drives increased kindness, utility, and creativity, enriching society directly.
- **Self-correcting.** Insular mutual-admiration circles stagnate, while outward generosity is amplified.

The result is a society where the primary incentive for surplus accumulation is *being a good force for others*.

---

### VI. Axiomatic Principles

1. **Outcome Primacy.** Basic material needs are rights, delivered directly, not dependent on market income.
2. **Ecological Containment.** Human material throughput is strictly subordinate to biosphere recovery.
3. **Equal Agency.** Every person receives an identical share of the surplus and an identical right to allocate half of it.
4. **Voluntary Relation.** All value beyond the core is distributed through a web of freely chosen recognition, not centralized redistribution.
5. **Dignity of Disengagement.** Full material security is preserved for those who opt out of the recognition economy entirely.

---

### Distinction from Universal Basic Income (UBI)

| Dimension | UBI | UBO |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Core Guarantee | Cash income | Direct provision of shelter, food, health, education |
| Surplus Allocation | Unrestricted cash, spend/save at will | 50% must be given away as recognition |
| Ecological Constraint | Not inherent | Hard cap defined by biodiversity recovery |
| Social Fabric | Individualistic | Structurally relational, proximity-reinforcing |
| Anti-Calcification Mechanism | None (market-driven) | Mandatory participation in esteem economy (with opt-out) |

---

This formal definition captures a system where the economy’s output serves three concentric purposes: **survival is unconditionally guaranteed**, **ecological boundaries are inviolable**, and **abundance is distributed through the radical act of thanking one another**.
