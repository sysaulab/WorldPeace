## The Core Insight

Money is not wealth. Wealth is:  
- Healthy land  
- Skilled hands  
- Trust between neighbors  
- Knowledge passed down  
- Time to care for children and elders  

Money is a **signal** of wealth. It works only when the signal is accurate and the loop is fast.

When money loses its signal (inflated, hoarded, detached from real contribution) → **paperweight**.  
When people have no signal (no money to coordinate, no price on value, no way to scale good work) → **monkey zoo**.

---

## The Three Laws (Revised)

### Law 1: Money must flow toward care, not away from it.

In a healthy system, when you do something useful for others, money flows toward you. When you do harm, money flows away (or you pay a cost).  
- **Paperweight violation:** Money flows to extractors, not caregivers. Dividends go to distant shareholders, not to the mother who raised the child.  
- **Monkey zoo violation:** Care happens but cannot be measured or rewarded. The best caregiver starves while the loudest chimp gets the banana.

**Your existing concept:** Feedback integrity. Money is the feedback.

### Law 2: People must be able to say "no" to money.

If money can buy anything, it will buy your dignity, your land, your vote, your child's future. A healthy society has **sacred zones** where money does not enter:  
- A judge's verdict.  
- A parent's love.  
- A citizen's vote.  
- A person's bodily integrity.

- **Paperweight violation:** Everything has a price. Nothing is sacred.  
- **Monkey zoo violation:** No money anywhere, so even useful exchanges become barter chaos.

**Your existing concept:** The membrane. It must block money from certain zones, while allowing it in others.

### Law 3: The velocity of money and the velocity of care must match.

If money circulates faster than care can be delivered, speculation dominates production. If care circulates faster than money can track, caregivers burn out unpaid.

The healthy state: every unit of care receives timely money, and every unit of money returns to care before it decays into hoarding.

**Your existing concept:** Energy thresholds and phase transitions. When velocity mismatch exceeds a threshold, the system tips into sickness.

## The Integrated Diagram (Text Version)

```
[People's Needs & Skills]
         │
         │ (work, creativity, cooperation)
         ▼
      [MONEY]
         │
         │ (wages, investment, prices)
         ▼
[People's Health & Capability]
         │
         │ (care, teaching, healing, building)
         ▼
      [MONEY]  (tip, tax, spend, lend)
         │
         │ (circulates back)
         ▼
[People's Needs & Skills]  (loop closes)
```

This loop is **healthy** when:
- Money changes hands at least 5-10 times per year (not hoarded).
- Each exchange is voluntary and transparent.
- Harm is priced in (polluter pays, abuser loses).
- Care is priced out (gift zones exist).

The loop is **paperweight** when:
- Money changes hands mostly between financial instruments (velocity near zero in real economy).
- Wages are suppressed while dividends soar.
- Externalized costs (pollution, community destruction) are not counted.

The loop is **monkey zoo** when:
- No agreed money exists (or it is constantly devalued).
- Barter is inefficient; trust is local and fragile.
- Good work cannot scale; bad work cannot be disciplined.

---

## The One Question That Diagnoses Any Economy

Ask: **Does the money in this system punish harm and reward care?**

- If yes → healthy (not perfect, but repairable).  
- If no → paperweight (money rewards extraction, punishes care).  
- If money is absent → monkey zoo (no signal at all).
