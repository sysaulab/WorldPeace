# The Unhijackable Truth

## A Proposal for a Decentralized Information System

**A Position Paper**

**Date:** March 5, 2026

**Author:** A Collaborative Inquiry

### Preamble: A Problem Stated is a Problem Half-Solved

We began this inquiry with a simple question: "Can you find me detailed reports of the actual damage observed by the two oreshnik strikes in ukraine?" The answer we received was not simple. It was a tapestry of contradictions, strategic silence, and information warfare. We learned that Russian sources claimed "serious destruction," while Western analysts posited the use of inert warheads for political signaling. We learned that the strikes were part of a larger assault, making it impossible to isolate the damage caused by a single weapon. We learned that the most detailed visual evidence was not of the strike itself, but of Ukraine's counter-strike on the missile's launch site—a site whose damage was then questioned by international analysts.

This single exchange is a microcosm of the human condition in the 21st century. It is a perfect, depressing illustration of the problem your vision seeks to solve.

We are drowning in information, yet starved for truth. We have built a global network that can transmit a video from a war zone to any screen on Earth in seconds, yet we cannot agree on the fundamental reality of what that video depicts. We have created a "fog of war" that is no longer a temporary byproduct of conflict, but a permanent, manufactured feature of it.

This paper is a call to action. It argues that the creation of an **open, distributed, and impossible-to-hijack information gathering organ** is not a futuristic fantasy, but an urgent necessity for the survival of informed consent, functional democracy, and perhaps, peace itself.

### 1. The Diagnosis: The Centralization of Reality

The core problem is not the existence of lies, but the architecture of our information ecosystem. For centuries, power over information was centralized. A king controlled the town crier. A state controlled the official gazette. In the 20th century, a handful of network news anchors and newspaper editors acted as gatekeepers of a shared, albeit imperfect, reality.

The internet promised to decentralize this power. Instead, it has led to a recentralization, more powerful and insidious than ever before. Today, reality is mediated by a handful of unaccountable algorithms owned by private corporations and manipulated by state actors. This architecture has several fatal flaws:

- **Ephemerality:** Information on centralized platforms is fragile. It can be deleted by a corporation's policy change, a government's takedown request, or a hacker's breach. The historical record is constantly being rewritten, not with ink, but with a simple `DELETE` command.
- **Malleability:** A video on a server is just data. Without a cryptographic chain of custody from the sensor (the camera) to the viewer, we have no way of verifying its provenance. Deepfakes are just the logical endpoint of this inherent untrustworthiness.
- **Monetization of Outrage:** Centralized platforms are not designed to surface the truth; they are designed to maximize engagement. The most divisive, emotionally charged, and often false content is the most profitable. The system is economically incentivized to lie.
- **Single Points of Failure:** A government that wishes to control a narrative need not control every citizen. It only needs to control the small number of platforms through which information flows, or to threaten the small number of journalists with access to those platforms.

This is the architecture that enabled the "fog of war" we witnessed in our inquiry. It is an architecture that makes propaganda not just possible, but inevitable.

### 2. The Vision: An Immune System for the Truth

What would an alternative architecture look like? It would not be a single website, app, or organization. It would be a new layer of the internet itself—a protocol for reality. It would be designed from the ground up with a single, non-negotiable principle: **the truth must be permanent, verifiable, and accessible to all.**

This system would act as an immune system for the global body politic. When a pathogen—a lie, a piece of disinformation, a manufactured narrative—enters the system, the immune system would not prevent it. It would do something far more powerful: it would provide the definitive, unalterable record against which the pathogen could be compared and identified.

Its core functions would be:

- **Immortality:** Once a piece of information enters the system, it can never be deleted or altered. Not by a government, not by a corporation, not by anyone. The record is final.
- **Provenance:** Every piece of information would carry with it a cryptographic seal of its origin—a signature from the sensor that captured it. You could verify not just the *what*, but the *who*, *when*, and *where* with mathematical certainty.
- **Transparency:** The entire system would be open source. Its code, its data structures, its methods of verification would be public for all to audit. Trust would not be placed in an institution, but in mathematics and code.
- **Resilience:** With no central servers or points of control, the system would be nearly impossible to shut down. It would be a distributed mesh of devices, each holding a piece of the whole, ensuring that the truth survives any attack.

### 3. The System in Action: Seeing Through the Fog

Let us return to our starting point: the Oreshnik strike on Lviv. In a world with this system, the sequence of events would be profoundly different.

- **Step 1: Immutable Capture.** A witness in Lviv films the aftermath. Their phone, running a background protocol, automatically hashes the video and signs it with its unique, hardware-based private key, creating a permanent, unforgeable record. This raw data is immediately propagated across the distributed network. It is now immortal. No government can seize it. No platform can delete it.

- **Step 2: Corroborated Context.** A commercial satellite passes over the Lviv industrial site. Its high-resolution imagery, signed by the satellite operator, is automatically added to the same distributed record, linked by time and location. Radiation sensors around the city publish their immutable readings. The event is no longer a single video; it is a constellation of permanently linked, independently verifiable data points.

- **Step 3: Transparent Analysis.** An independent analyst in Brazil downloads all this raw data. They publish their conclusion—"the missile carried an inert warhead"—and, crucially, they publish it *as a link to the raw data they used to reach that conclusion*. Their analysis is now another immutable piece of information, permanently linked to the evidence. A state-affiliated analyst publishes a competing conclusion, linking to the same raw data but highlighting different details. Both analyses exist forever, side-by-side.

- **Step 4: Collective Sensemaking.** A decentralized reputation layer, built on top of this immutable data, begins to take shape. Users and organizations build trust over time based on the accuracy of their analyses. The Brazilian analyst, with a long history of verifiable work, has a high reputation. The state-affiliated analyst's reputation is low. The system does not *tell* you what to believe. It provides you with all the raw evidence, all the competing analyses, and the transparent reputation scores of the analysts, allowing you, the citizen of the world, to make an informed judgment.

In this world, the "fog of war" does not disappear. But it becomes transparent. You can see the fog for what it is—a collection of competing claims, each tethered to evidence. Propaganda becomes an exercise in futility because its claims can be instantly, permanently, and publicly checked against an immutable record of reality.

### 4. A Credible Path: From Vision to Reality

This vision is not a utopian dream. The technological building blocks exist today. As we have discovered, projects like **Iroh** provide a networking and data synchronization layer designed for exactly this kind of decentralized, resilient system. It offers a credible path to implementation.

- **Iroh-blobs** can provide the permanent, content-addressed storage for raw video, audio, and documents.
- **Iroh-docs** can manage the dynamic, synchronizable state of events, linking together different pieces of evidence and analysis as they emerge.
- **Iroh's core networking** can create the direct, peer-to-peer mesh that makes the system censorship-resistant.
- **Emerging standards for zero-knowledge proofs** can provide a privacy-preserving identity layer, allowing for cryptographic attestation of source and location without enabling mass surveillance.

The challenge is no longer technological. It is a challenge of will, of coordination, and of funding. It is the challenge of convincing a critical mass of humanity that the current architecture is a threat to our collective future, and that a better one is not only possible, but necessary.

### 5. Conclusion: The Choice Before Us

We stand at a crossroads. On one path lies the continued centralization of reality, where a handful of algorithms and state actors determine what we see, what we believe, and ultimately, what we fight for. This is a path toward a permanent, inescapable fog of war.

On the other path lies the construction of a new infrastructure for truth. It is a path that requires us to believe that humanity is capable of building tools that empower, rather than enslave. It is a path that asks us to replace faith in institutions with faith in open, verifiable systems.

The need for such a system is not academic. It is critical. It is the difference between a world where support for war can be manufactured by propaganda, and a world where the decision to go to war must be made in the cold, clear light of a permanent, public, and undeniable record. The choice is ours. The time to build is now.
