# New Levant

## The Authorization to Love

**What if the solution has been hidden in plain sight all along?**

Not in the corridors of power, where treaties are signed and broken. Not in the military strategies, where victories are measured in bodies. Not even in the peace conferences, where hope goes to die slowly.

What if the solution is simply this: **that we are permitted to love each other, and that love, when structured properly, builds economies that make war unthinkable?**

For one hundred years, we have been told a lie. We have been told that loving the other means betraying the self. That a Jew who sees the face of God in a Palestinian is a traitor to his people. That a Muslim who breaks bread with an Israeli is a sellout to her cause.

This is the lie. And it is a lie because it contradicts the deepest truth of both our traditions.

The Torah commands: "Love your neighbor as yourself" (Leviticus 19:18). The Prophet taught: "None of you has faith until he loves for his brother what he loves for himself." These are not metaphors. They are not poetry for the distant future. They are instructions. They are permissions. They are **authorizations**.

We have been authorized to love. We simply forgot. Or rather, we were taught to forget by those who profit from our hatred.

But here is the truth that no army can kill and no wall can contain: **love is the prerequisite. Everything else is just architecture.**

The "Welcome Home" Hosting Initiative is not a program. It is a **permission slip**. It is the space where Israelis and Palestinians look at each other across a kitchen table and remember that the other is not an abstraction, not a statistic, not an enemy—but a human being, made in the image of God, deserving of dignity and home.

Only after that permission is claimed—only after the lies have dissolved in the light of proximity—can we build the Levant Economic Peace Zone. The economy is the body. Love is the soul. The body without the soul is a corpse.

Let us begin with the soul.

## A Prerequisite for Lasting Change

## Executive Summary

The "Welcome Home" Hosting Initiative is a grassroots, human-centered program designed to address the fundamental obstacle to peace: the dehumanization of the other through decades of propaganda, trauma, and separation.

The initiative facilitates the voluntary hosting of Palestinian families by Israeli families within Israel's internationally recognized borders (pre-1967 lines) for defined periods. This is not charity. This is not tourism. This is **reparation made intimate**—an opportunity for displaced families to connect with the land of their ancestors, and for Israeli families to meet the humans behind the headlines.

The program is explicitly framed as the **first stage** of a two-stage process. It does not solve the political conflict. It creates the human conditions under which political solutions become possible. It is the love that will allow the economy to grow.

## 1. The Problem: Why Peace Agreements Fail

Every major peace initiative between Israelis and Palestinians has failed not because the maps were wrong, but because the **people were not ready**.

- The Oslo Accords (1993) were signed on the White House lawn by leaders who shook hands. Within years, the agreement collapsed in a hail of suicide bombs and military reprisals. Why? Because the populations on both sides had been prepared by decades of propaganda to see the other as an enemy, not a partner.
- The Camp David Summit (2000) failed when Arafat and Barak could not bridge the gap on Jerusalem and refugees. But the gap was not just political. It was psychological. There was no reservoir of mutual trust to draw upon when negotiations got hard.
- The Gaza Disengagement (2005) saw Israel remove settlements unilaterally. Rather than building trust, it created a vacuum filled by Hamas. Why? Because there was no human relationship between Gazans and Israelis to give the withdrawal meaning.

The pattern is clear: **political agreements require human foundations.** You cannot build a house on sand. You cannot build peace on hatred.

## 2. The Solution: Human Proximity as Infrastructure

The "Welcome Home" Initiative is based on a simple, empirically validated insight: **proximity dismantles lies.**

When an Israeli family hosts a Palestinian family in their home, the abstractions collapse. The Palestinian is no longer "the enemy" or "the refugee" or "the terrorist." She becomes a mother who worries about her children's education, a father who tells jokes, a grandmother who makes coffee the way your grandmother makes coffee.

When a Palestinian family stays in an Israeli home, the Israeli is no longer "the occupier" or "the settler" or "the soldier." He becomes a man who fears for his family's safety, a woman who volunteers at the local clinic, a child who loves the same cartoons your child loves.

This is not sentimentality. This is **cognitive restructuring**. The brain cannot hold two conflicting ideas indefinitely. When you know someone personally, the stereotype dies.

## 3. Program Design

**3.1 The Core Concept**

Selected Israeli host families invite a Palestinian family—currently displaced and living in the West Bank, Gaza Strip, or diaspora—to stay in their home for a defined period (initially two to four weeks, with potential for extension).

During this time, the Palestinian family has the opportunity to:
- Connect with the land, visiting the sites of their ancestral villages (where possible).
- Build a personal relationship with an Israeli family.
- Experience daily life in Israel—the schools, the markets, the synagogues, the beaches.
- Share their own story, their own food, their own culture.

The Israeli family has the opportunity to:
- Meet Palestinians as guests, not as subjects of occupation.
- Hear firsthand accounts of displacement and longing.
- Share their own fears, their own history, their own hopes.
- Discover that hospitality is a mitzvah and a sunnah—a sacred act in both traditions.

**3.2 Guiding Principles**

**Voluntary Participation:** All participation is purely voluntary, rooted in a desire for reconciliation and justice. No one is coerced.

**Human Dignity:** The Palestinian family is framed not as guests or beneficiaries of charity, but as individuals returning to a homeland to which they have deep historical and emotional ties. The Israeli family acts not as a "host" in the colonial sense, but as a partner in a shared human experience.

**Psychological Preparation:** Before any hosting takes place, both families undergo extensive joint preparatory workshops led by trauma-informed facilitators (such as those from organizations like the Parents Circle-Families Forum). These workshops address:
- Historical narratives (the Holocaust, the Nakba, the wars).
- Personal trauma and its triggers.
- The emotional challenges of the encounter.
- Communication skills for difficult conversations.

**Ongoing Support:** During the hosting period, a dedicated support team is available 24/7 to address any issues that arise—cultural misunderstandings, emotional distress, logistical problems.

**Post-Hosting Connection:** The relationship does not end when the visit concludes. Families are supported in maintaining connection through video calls, return visits (where possible), and joint participation in community events.

**3.3 Focus on Pre-1967 Israel**

Hosting takes place within the Green Line (Israel's internationally recognized borders). This choice is deliberate:

- It focuses the reparative act on the land where the majority of 1948 refugees originated.
- It avoids the political complexities of the West Bank settlements.
- It allows the program to proceed even in the absence of a final-status agreement.

This is not to endorse or reject any particular political outcome. It is simply to create a space where human connection can occur without being immediately consumed by political controversy.

## 4. The Moral Grounding: One Tradition, Two Tastes

The "Welcome Home" Initiative is not a secular do-gooder project. It is deeply rooted in the moral codes of both Judaism and Islam.

**4.1 Hospitality (*Hachnasat Orchim / Diyafa*)**

**Judaism:**
- Abraham's tent was open on all four sides to welcome strangers (Genesis 18). The rabbis teach that Abraham's hospitality was greater than his prophecy.
- The Talmud states: "Hospitality to wayfarers is greater than welcoming the Divine Presence" (Shabbat 127a).
- The commandment to love the stranger appears 36 times in the Torah—more than any other commandment.

**Islam:**
- The Prophet Muhammad said: "Whoever believes in Allah and the Last Day, let him honor his guest" (Bukhari and Muslim).
- The Quran describes the righteous as those who "give food, despite their love for it, to the poor, the orphan, and the captive" (Quran 76:8).
- In Arab culture, hospitality is not merely custom; it is honor. To harm a guest is unthinkable.

**Application:** When an Israeli family opens their home to a Palestinian family, they are not performing a political act. They are performing a **sacred act**. They are embodying Abraham. They are honoring the Prophet. They are fulfilling the deepest command of their faith.

**4.2 Welcoming the Stranger (*Ger / Ibn al-Sabil*)**

**Judaism:**
- "The stranger who resides with you shall be to you as one of your citizens; you shall love him as yourself, for you were strangers in the land of Egypt" (Leviticus 19:34).
- The experience of being strangers in Egypt is foundational to Jewish identity. To forget it is to forget who we are.

**Islam:**
- The Quran commands kindness to "the near relative, the orphan, the needy, the traveler in need [*ibn al-sabil*], and the beggar" (Quran 2:177).
- The *ibn al-sabil* (literally "son of the road") is entitled to support from the community.

**Application:** The Palestinian family is not an intruder. They are the stranger the Torah commands us to love. They are the traveler the Quran commands us to support.

**4.3 Justice (*Tzedek / Adl*)**

**Judaism:**
- "Justice, justice shall you pursue" (Deuteronomy 16:20). The repetition teaches that justice must characterize both the means and the ends.
- *Tzedakah* (often translated as charity) actually means justice. The poor have a right to support.

**Islam:**
- "Indeed, Allah commands you to render trusts to whom they are due and when you judge between people to judge with justice" (Quran 4:58).
- *Adl* is one of God's names. To act justly is to imitate God.

**Application:** The "Welcome Home" program is not charity. It is justice. It acknowledges, on a human level, the injustice of displacement. It does not solve the political question of return. But it says: "We see you. We acknowledge your loss. We will share our home as a first step toward sharing this land."

## 5. The Hatred Precondition: Why This Must Come First

In the earlier proposal, we established that no sustainable peace can be negotiated until the propaganda of hatred has stopped. The "Welcome Home" Initiative is the mechanism for stopping it.

**5.1 How Propaganda Works**

Propaganda does not simply lie. It **depersonalizes**. It turns human beings into symbols, statistics, threats. The Israeli becomes "the occupier." The Palestinian becomes "the terrorist." These labels are not neutral descriptions; they are **permission slips for hatred**.

When you call someone a name long enough, you forget they have a name.

**5.2 How Proximity Heals**

The "Welcome Home" Initiative reverses this process. It **re-personalizes** the other. The Israeli learns that the Palestinian has a name—Amina, Khalil, Fatima. The Palestinian learns that the Israeli has a name—David, Sarah, Moshe.

Once you know someone's name, it becomes much harder to hate their people.

This is not speculation. It is documented psychology. The "contact hypothesis" in social psychology, first developed by Gordon Allport in 1954, holds that under appropriate conditions (equal status, common goals, institutional support), interpersonal contact is one of the most effective ways to reduce prejudice.

**5.3 The Prerequisite for Stage Two**

The Levant Economic Peace Zone cannot function if the people entering it still believe the lies. You cannot build a cooperative enterprise with someone you think wants to kill you. You cannot share a bank with someone you have been taught to despise.

The "Welcome Home" Initiative creates the **human infrastructure** for the economic zone. It builds the trust, the relationships, the mutual understanding that make economic cooperation possible and durable.

Without Stage One, Stage Two is just another development project—well-intentioned, well-funded, and destined to fail.

With Stage One, Stage Two becomes **love made visible**—an economy that grows from the soil of human connection.

## 6. Program Phases

**6.1 Phase One: Pilot (Years 1-2)**

- Recruit 50 Israeli host families and 50 Palestinian guest families.
- Conduct intensive preparatory workshops.
- Facilitate hosting periods of 2-4 weeks.
- Document outcomes through interviews, surveys, and ethnographic research.
- Refine program design based on lessons learned.

**6.2 Phase Two: Expansion (Years 3-5)**

- Scale to 500 families per year.
- Develop alumni network for continued connection and advocacy.
- Begin training host families from the alumni pool to become trainers for new participants.
- Establish partnerships with schools, synagogues, and mosques to broaden reach.

**6.3 Phase Three: Integration (Years 6-10)**

- By this point, thousands of families have participated.
- Alumni become a political constituency for peace.
- The relationships built in homes become the foundation for the Levant Economic Peace Zone.
- Families who hosted each other are prioritized for joint business licenses and residency in the zone.

## 7. Measuring Success

The "Welcome Home" Initiative is designed for empirical evaluation. We propose the following metrics:

**Primary Outcomes:**
- Reduction in prejudice scores (measured by standardized instruments before and after participation).
- Increase in trust scores between participants.
- Continued contact between families after the formal hosting period ends.
- Willingness to recommend the program to others.

**Secondary Outcomes:**
- Changes in political attitudes (support for compromise, recognition of the other's narrative).
- Participation in alumni activities and advocacy.
- Interest in participating in Stage Two (the economic zone).

**Long-Term Outcomes:**
- Reduction in support for violence among participants.
- Increased intergroup contact in participants' social networks.
- Diffusion of positive attitudes to non-participating family and friends.

## 8. Addressing the Challenges

**8.1 Security Concerns**

**Challenge:** Hosting a family from the "other side" will be viewed by some as a security risk. Extremists may threaten participants.

**Response:** 
- The program operates transparently, with the knowledge of relevant authorities.
- Host families receive security training and have access to 24/7 support.
- The program's moral grounding in both traditions provides a measure of protection—attacking someone for practicing hospitality is attacking the core of both faiths.
- Participants are encouraged to build community support, so they are not isolated.

**8.2 Psychological Trauma**

**Challenge:** For Palestinian families, entering an Israeli home may evoke feelings of vulnerability and anger. For Israeli families, hosting Palestinians may trigger fears rooted in years of conflict.

**Response:**
- Extensive psychological preparation before hosting.
- Trauma-informed facilitators available throughout.
- Post-hosting debriefing and support.
- Option to withdraw at any time without stigma.

**8.3 Political Exploitation**

**Challenge:** Opponents may frame the program as "normalization" that lets Israel off the hook, or as a trick to undermine the Right of Return.

**Response:**
- The program's literature is explicit: this is a human process, not a political settlement. It does not solve the Right of Return; it creates the human conditions under which that question can be addressed with dignity.
- The program partners with respected organizations on both sides who can vouch for its integrity.
- Participants are encouraged to maintain their political commitments while engaging humanly with the other.

**8.4 Scale**

**Challenge:** Hosting thousands of families cannot address the displacement of millions.

**Response:**
- The program is a pilot, a proof of concept. It demonstrates what is possible.
- If successful, it becomes a model that can be adapted and replicated.
- Its primary purpose is not to solve the refugee crisis, but to create the human relationships that make political solutions possible.
