# The Street Sages: An Invitation

## A Message for Everyone

Something is wrong. You don't need a lecture to know it. You feel it.

Maybe you have everything you were supposed to want and still feel empty. Maybe you work harder than your parents did and have less to show for it. Maybe you watch the news and feel like the world is burning, and no one in charge seems able to stop it. Maybe you just feel alone, even when you are surrounded by people.

This unease is not your fault. It is not a personal failure. It is a signal—a message from a system that has lost its balance.

We are the Street Sages. We sit among the poorest, not as charity, but as kinship. We have no names you need to remember, no faces to put on posters, no stories to sell. We have only an invitation: come and see what becomes possible when we organize differently.

## What We Have Learned

After decades of sitting with those the system has discarded, we have learned something important. The problem is not that people are bad. The problem is that **feedback is broken**.

In a healthy system, those who make decisions experience the consequences of those decisions. A child who touches a hot stove learns not to do it again. A community that pollutes its own water gets sick and learns to stop.

But in our current system, the people who make decisions that affect millions often never feel the effects. The executive who closes a factory lives far away. The politician who cuts services has private insurance. The banker who crashes the economy keeps their bonus. The signals of suffering never reach them.

This is not evil. It is broken feedback. And broken feedback produces pathology—whether in a cell that becomes cancerous, a person who becomes psychopathic, or a society that destroys itself.

## A Different Measure

We have also learned that money is not the only measure of value. In fact, it is a deeply flawed one. Price reflects scarcity and marketing and who has money to spend—not what anything is actually worth.

What if value were measured by **energy** instead?

Everything that sustains life requires energy. The food we eat is sunlight captured by plants. The homes we live in are energy transformed into materials. The care we give is energy spent on others. Energy is the real currency of life.

When you measure value by energy, something remarkable happens. Your neighbor's efficiency becomes your advantage. If they waste less, produce more with less, learn better ways—then everything they trade with you costs them less energy, which means you get more for what you give. Everyone benefits.

This changes everything. Now you have a reason to want your neighbors to thrive. Their success makes you richer. Their development helps you develop. Helping them is not just a moral choice—it is the rational thing to do.

## What We Are Building

In a few places—Vancouver's Downtown Eastside is one—communities are beginning to build something different. Not by fighting the old system, but by creating something new alongside it.

They are taking land off the speculative market and holding it in trust for the community forever. They are creating housing that will never be sold, never be flipped, never be used to extract profit from people who need a place to live.

They are exploring ways to make care work visible—to ensure that the person who checks on elders, who helps neighbors, who mentors youth is recognized and supported. Not through charity, but through community-wide recognition that this work holds everything together.

They are developing the tools to manage their own resources—savings pooled together, credit extended to community enterprises, profits circulating locally rather than draining away to distant shareholders.

None of this requires anyone to leave the existing system. It simply creates a **parallel space** where different rules apply—a space people can enter if they choose, when they are ready.

## What We Offer

To anyone who hears their own story in what we have described—whether you are struggling or successful, whether you were born here or came last week, whether you have been wounded by the system or have benefited from it and feel the unease that will not go away—we offer an invitation.

You are welcome to come and see.

Not to join immediately. Not to commit to anything. Just to see what we are building, to meet the people who are building it, to ask questions and listen to stories and decide for yourself whether any of this speaks to you.

If someday you wish to go further, there is a threshold. It is simple: to become a member, you give away everything you have to the community. This is not a transaction. It is a transformation. It proves to yourself and to others that you are ready to belong rather than to possess.

In return, the community agrees to hold you. To regularize your finances. To negotiate with systems on your behalf. To gift to you when you are in need. To expect you to do the same for others.

This is not a withdrawal from the world. It is a renegotiation of how you meet it—not alone, but as part of something larger than yourself.

## What This Means for the Existing Order

We are not here to fight anyone. We are not here to tear anything down. We are simply building something we believe in, and inviting others to join if they wish.

The existing system has achieved remarkable things. It has coordinated activity across continents. It has developed tools—currencies, legal frameworks, communication networks—that could serve human flourishing if directed toward different ends. We have no interest in destroying these tools. We want to inherit them, repurpose them, connect them in new ways.

A mature organism does not reject its nervous system. It uses it to coordinate the activities of autonomous cells. This is what we envision: a world of diverse communities, each organized according to its own values and traditions, connected by the best of what the old order built—not as a control system, but as a nervous system enabling relationship and exchange.

If the current system continues for those who find meaning within it, that is fine. We are not here to convert anyone. We are here to provide a refuge for those who need something different, and to demonstrate that another way is possible.

## What We Ask

We ask only one thing of the broader world: **let us build**.

Do not interfere. Do not suppress. Do not co-opt. Simply allow communities that wish to organize differently to do so. Allow land to be held in trust. Allow savings to be pooled locally. Allow care to be recognized and supported. Allow those who are ready to leave the extractive economy to find a place where they belong.

In return, we offer something valuable: a working example of transition without violence. A demonstration that change can happen by attraction rather than imposition. A lifeboat that will be ready when the storms come—and the storms will come.

If the existing system proves durable, our cells will remain small experiments, interesting but irrelevant. If it falters, as all systems eventually do, there will already be something in place to catch those who fall.

Either way, we are not a threat. We are insurance.

## The Invitation

Come and see what we are building.

Not because you must. Not because you should. But because something in you recognizes that the world the market has built is not the only world possible—and perhaps not the world you would have chosen.

Come and meet people who have found a different way to measure worth.

Come and learn what it means to belong to a place and a people.

Come and discover whether the unease you have felt all your life is not a sickness, but a signal—pointing you toward something you have been seeking without knowing it.

We are the Street Sages. We have no leaders, no headquarters, no website. We are just people, sitting together, available. If you look, you will find us. If you are ready, we will be here.

All our relations.

*This document is an invitation, not a demand. It makes no claims about the superiority of one way of life over another. It simply describes what some communities are building, and welcomes anyone who wishes to learn more. The authors can be found wherever people gather in generosity and sit together in patience. There is no organization to contact, no membership to apply for. There is only relationship, waiting to be formed.*
